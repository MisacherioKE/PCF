Who We Are

Pillars Care Foundation is an independent registered non-governmental, non-political, non-religious and non-profit
civil society based In Migori – Kenya East Africa.

Our Vision and Mission

Our Vision

Creation of a healthy empowered youth population participating in agribusiness, environmental conservation
and sustainable development.

Our Mission

Contributing to the education of young people, through a value system based, to help build a better world
where people are self-fulfilled as individuals and play constructive role in rehabilitating the Environment
and restoring the youths back to Agriculture.

What We Do

  • Advocate and campaign for greater recognition, funding and opportunities for nature-based activities.

  • Provide expert advice, support and a free membership scheme to support communities to grow in sustainable ways.

  • Design and deliver innovative training programs which empower and enable communities to thrive and grow.

  • Forge pathways for our members to access commissioned services and demonstrate that they deliver high quality provision.

Improving People's Lives

Many people assume that community gardens and city or care farms are just nice places to visit where plants are grown and animals kept. But that is far from the whole picture.

Many of them also offer an amazing array of benefits and opportunities, which can include education programs, play schemes, healthy living initiatives, work and skills training, social enterprises, volunteer opportunities, environmental schemes, horticultural therapy groups, facilities for people with disabilities…the list goes on and on!

Our History

Together, we have a combined history of over 3 years’ experience in advocacy, lobbying, membership growth, support and capacity building, and working with sector partners. By merging, we are able to offer a range of services for members, government and the wider sector. A key strength of Pillars Care is that we work with and support a diverse membership base across the whole of the Kenya, working across both urban and rural settings.

Our members operate across a wide spectrum, offering services including Green Care, community and social based activities. There is often considerable crossover in delivery different settings. For example, a community farm may be involved in both community development and care farming activities. By focusing on the services or activities delivered or facilitated by our members, rather than the physical form upon which our members operate (e.g. a farm, garden, school, orchard), we aim to widen the opportunities open to our members and the wider sector.
